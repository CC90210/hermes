The loop Hermes closes

Five steps. Zero touch. Under a second.

When a PO arrives, Hermes runs the whole workflow himself. You see a summary in the morning, not a to-do list.

STEP 01
📧 PO Received
Hermes watches your inbox. Detects incoming POs by subject, attachment type, or body content. Grabs the PDF, Excel, or EDI X12 file.
STEP 02
🤖 PO Parsed
Hermes reads the document and extracts the PO number, customer, line items, prices, and ship dates. Writes them into a structured order record.
STEP 03
📦 Order Entered
Hermes enters the order into A2000 via EDI 850 or the vendor API. He also sends an EDI 855 PO acknowledgment back to the buyer within the 24-hour window.
STEP 04
🏷️ Labels + ASN
GS1-128 carton labels print to your Zebra thermal printer. The EDI 856 ASN transmits four hours before the DC cutoff. Walgreens never fines you for a missed window.
STEP 05
🧾 Invoice Out
The invoice generates from A2000, gets attached to an email, and goes back to the buyer. Every action is logged. You never touched a keyboard.
When everything is clean, you see one line in your morning briefing: "7 POs processed overnight, $48,220 total, all clean."

Why your data stays yours

Think of Hermes as a digital filing cabinet.

THE PLAIN-ENGLISH VERSION
Your business data lives in a vault on your computer, locked with military-grade encryption. The vault is backed up automatically every six hours to a separate, encrypted location, so nothing is ever lost. You're the only one with the key. When Hermes needs to think — to read a PO, to draft an email — he asks an AI provider through a legally binding agreement that says: process this, then forget it. Don't store it. Don't train on it. Don't share it.

The cloud isn't the risk. The risk is unaccountable storage. We use providers that are SOC 2 Type II certified, sign Data Processing Agreements with legal teeth, and have a financial reason to honor them — if they leaked your data, they'd be sued out of existence. That's a stronger safety net than most local IT setups.

🔐 Two-key encryption

Your data and your credentials are protected by a two-layer encryption scheme. Even if someone intercepted the transfer, they'd see scrambled gibberish — and even if they got the file off your machine, they'd still need a second key to read it.

🗝️ Only you hold the keys

Encryption keys live on your machine, in a file only your Windows account can read. We don't have a copy. No master key, no backdoor, no recovery handshake that bypasses you.

💾 Encrypted backups every 6 hours

Automated, encrypted snapshots of your order history and audit log, written to a separate provider so a single failure can't take you down. You can roll back any change, any day.

📜 No-storage, no-training contracts

The AI providers we use (Anthropic, OpenAI) are bound by Data Processing Agreements: they cannot store your data, train on it, or share it. If they did, they'd be in breach and exposed to lawsuits in the millions.

📋 Full audit trail

Every action Hermes takes — every PO read, every order entered, every email drafted — is logged with timestamp and reason. You can replay any decision he made, any time.

🛑 Fail-stopped, not fail-open

When Hermes is unsure, he pauses and alerts you. He never guesses on a real order. The pipeline stops before a bad action happens, not after.

If you ever want to take the cloud out of the equation entirely — run the AI on a machine in your own office, fully offline — that's a simple upgrade we can do later. It's not required for the trial, and it's not required for the production system to be safe.
